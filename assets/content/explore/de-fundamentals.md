## Overview

Data engineering fundamentals cover how data moves from source systems into models and products that teams can trust. Before orchestration tools or cloud platforms, the core ideas are ingestion, transformation, storage, and delivery.

Strong fundamentals make every later decision easier — whether you are choosing batch vs. streaming, designing a warehouse schema, or debugging a failed pipeline at 2 a.m.

---

## Core building blocks

- Ingestion: APIs, files, databases, event streams, and change-data-capture patterns.
- Transformation: cleaning, joining, aggregating, and applying business rules.
- Storage: warehouses, lakes, lakehouses, and when to use each.
- Modeling: staging, intermediate, and mart layers with clear grain and keys.
- Serving: APIs, BI semantic models, and reverse ETL to operational tools.

## What to learn next

Once fundamentals are solid, move into orchestration (Airflow, Dagster), distributed processing (Spark), and architecture patterns like medallion and data mesh — each builds directly on the concepts above.
